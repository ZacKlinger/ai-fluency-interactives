The human body contains several organ systems. The circulatory system pumps blood through the body. The respiratory system helps us breathe. These systems work together to keep the body functioning. Each organ system has specific organs that carry out its functions.

Add the grade and topic:
Write a reading passage about the circulatory and respiratory systems for 7th grade science.
AI output:

The circulatory and respiratory systems are two of the most important organ systems in the human body. The circulatory system consists of the heart, blood vessels, and blood, which work together to transport oxygen and nutrients to cells throughout the body. The respiratory system, which includes the lungs and airways, is responsible for gas exchange. When you breathe in, oxygen enters the lungs and is transferred to the bloodstream. Carbon dioxide, a waste product, is removed when you exhale.

Still missing: Reading level. Student vocabulary range. What students will do with this text. It is a competent paragraph. It is not a teaching tool.

Specify the output format:
Write a one-page informational text on how the circulatory and respiratory systems work together for 7th grade science. Bold and define five vocabulary terms in context. Use short paragraphs.
AI output:

Your heart and lungs are in constant conversation. Every time your lungs take in air, they are sending a message to your blood: there is oxygen ready for pickup. And every time your heart beats, it is answering: the delivery is on its way.

This exchange depends on two organ systems working as partners. The respiratory system (the network of airways and lungs that moves air in and out of your body) brings oxygen in and pushes carbon dioxide out. The circulatory system (the heart, blood, and blood vessels) carries that oxygen to every cell in your body and returns carbon dioxide to the lungs to be exhaled.

Getting closer - but missing: Who these students are. What they already know. What they are building toward. The text is cleaner, but it could belong to any 7th grade classroom anywhere.

Write a one-page informational text on how the circulatory and respiratory systems work together for 7th grade science. Bold and define five vocabulary terms in context. Use short paragraphs and concrete, familiar analogies. Students are reading between 3rd and 9th grade level and the text should build toward a claim-evidence-reasoning writing task.
AI output:

Think of your circulatory system as a delivery service and your respiratory system as the warehouse that keeps it stocked.

The respiratory system (the network of airways, lungs, and muscles that moves air in and out of your body) is where your blood picks up its cargo. When you breathe in, tiny air sacs called alveoli (small pouches in your lungs where oxygen and carbon dioxide are exchanged) act like loading docks. Oxygen passes through their thin walls directly into your blood.

From there, the circulatory system (the heart, blood vessels, and blood that move materials through your body) takes over. Your heart pumps oxygen-rich blood outward through arteries (blood vessels that carry blood away from the heart) and receives it back through veins (blood vessels that return blood to the heart). Every cell in your body gets a delivery. Every cell sends its waste - carbon dioxide - back the same way.

Almost there - but missing: How this text fits the teacher's voice. The specific summative task students are working toward. That last mile of judgment that only she has.

The full description - product, process, performance:
Write a one-page informational text on how the circulatory and respiratory systems interact for 7th grade science, aligned to NGSS. Bold and define five vocabulary terms in context. Use short paragraphs and concrete, familiar analogies. Students are reading between 3rd and 9th grade level; aim for 6th grade Flesch-Kincaid. Build toward a claim-evidence-reasoning task - include one sentence near the end that explicitly models a claim supported by evidence. Act as a curriculum collaborator, not a textbook.
AI output:

Think of your circulatory system as a delivery service and your respiratory system as the warehouse that keeps it stocked. They work so closely together that disrupting one immediately affects the other - which is exactly what makes them worth studying as a pair.

The respiratory system (the network of airways, lungs, and muscles that moves air in and out of your body) is where your blood picks up oxygen. Tiny pouches called alveoli (air sacs in your lungs built for gas exchange) act like loading docks. Oxygen passes through their thin walls directly into your bloodstream. Carbon dioxide - the waste your cells produce - travels the reverse route, crossing back into the alveoli to be exhaled.

The circulatory system (your heart, blood, and blood vessels working together to move materials through your body) carries that oxygen to every cell. Your heart pumps oxygen-rich blood outward through arteries and receives it back through veins once cells have used what they need.

Here is the claim the evidence supports: when your breathing rate increases during exercise, your heart rate increases too - because the two systems are calibrated to each other. More oxygen demand means more delivery, which means more pickup. You cannot push one system harder without pushing them both.
