## Key content summary (extracted)

### Lesson structure (all 8 modules)
Each lesson follows: Objectives -> Video -> Interactive -> Key Takeaways -> Exercise -> Reflection

### Module learning objectives

**Module 1 - Intro to AI Fluency**
- Define what AI Fluency means
- Articulate your values and constraints for more effective AI collaboration
- Identify your objectives for integrating AI tools

**Module 2 - AI Capabilities & Limits**
- Define generative AI and how it differs from other AI types
- Recognize key characteristics and technological foundations
- Identify major capabilities and limitations of current generative AI

**Module 3 - Pedagogy, AI & You**
- Recognize differences between model personalities
- Build your personal AI Constitution

**Module 4 - The 4D Framework**
- Explain how the 4D Framework applies to your work
- Define each of the 4Ds: Delegation, Description, Discernment, Diligence

**Module 5 - Ethical Focus (Delegation & Diligence)**
- Apply Delegation to student data decisions
- Practice Diligence through validation
- Protect student privacy (FERPA, district-approved tools, de-identification)

**Module 6 - Quality Focus (Description & Discernment)**
- Use Description skills to guide AI in creating differentiated instructional materials
- Use Discernment skills to evaluate AI-generated materials for accuracy and alignment

**Module 7 - Tying It All Together**
- Apply all four Ds in a single real workflow
- Navigate between loops dynamically
- Own the full process

**Module 8 - Closure & Enrichment**
- Certificate of completion
- Anthropic Academy resources

### Key scenario: Ms. Okafor (used in Modules 5 & 6)
7th grade science teacher. Students read between 3rd-9th grade level. 4 IEP students.
Cluster of multilingual learners. Building toward a claim-evidence-reasoning (CER) task.
Teaching circulatory and respiratory systems, aligned to NGSS.
Uses AI to draft differentiated informational text -- then applies Discernment checklist:
- Is the science accurate?
- Is the reading level right?
- Are the vocabulary words the ones that matter?
- Does the passage set up the summative CER task?

### Key quotes for interactive copy

"AI fluency: the ability to work with AI systems in ways that are effective, efficient, ethical, and safe."

"Description behaviors are the most common across all AI Fluency areas."
"Discernment behaviors are observed much more rarely -- it is the step most people skip."

"AI assistance does not transfer that responsibility."

"The work is yours. AI is a tool in service of it."

"Not just 'can AI do this' but 'should AI do this, and does it serve my students'?"

### Exercises (for interactive design context)

Module 1 Ex 2: "If AI could help me _______, I would be able to spend more time _______"
Module 2 Ex: Test AI on something you know cold -- Chat 1: versatility, Chat 2: hallucination, Chat 3: knowledge cutoff
Module 4 Ex 1: Map your AI questions to the 4D Framework
Module 5 Ex: Validate AI against data you already analyzed and know the answers to
Module 6 Ex: Differentiated Materials Challenge -- use Description + Discernment on a real upcoming lesson
Module 7 Ex: End-to-end 4D workflow on a real task from your own work
